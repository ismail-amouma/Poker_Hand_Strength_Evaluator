Card Notation Guide

In poker notation, each card is represented by a combination of rank and suit.

The rank of the card is represented by a number or letter:

  • 2 stands for the number 2
  • 3 stands for the number 3
  • 4 stands for the number 4
  • ... and so on up to 10
  • T stands for 10 (commonly used in notation)
  • J stands for Jack
  • Q stands for Queen
  • K stands for King
  • A stands for Ace

The suit of the card is represented by the first letter of the suit:

  • H stands for Hearts
  • D stands for Diamonds
  • S stands for Spades
  • C stands for Clubs

Combine the rank and suit to represent a card. For example:

  • 2H stands for the 2 of Hearts
  • 3D stands for the 3 of Diamonds
  • 6S stands for the 6 of Spades
  • 8C stands for the 8 of Clubs
  • AD stands for the Ace of Diamonds
